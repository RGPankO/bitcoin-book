## Key Data Points (extracted from transcript)

### House Price Story
- Eila (107 years old), born 1918, bought house in 1960 for £250
- Location: Whitehaven, central England (mining area)
- Average male weekly wage in 1960: £14/week → £730/year
- House cost = ~0.34 years salary (subsidized) or ~0.5 years at market price (£300-400)
- Today same house: £144,000-175,000 (~£150,000)
- Average UK salary today: £40,000/year
- House cost today = 3.5-4 years salary
- **7x less affordable** than 60 years ago

### UK vs US Affordability
- London boroughs: house price > 5x annual income (since 2000+)
- UK as whole: significantly worse than US
- US metro areas: mixed, some still under 3x income ratio
- Bulgaria comparison: at 1500 lv/month salary, 3-year income = ~€27,000

### Official vs Real Inflation
- Official inflation calculator: £250 (1960) = £5,000 today
- Real purchasing power (Bank of England): £7,300
- But actual house value went from £250 → £150,000 = **600x** nominal increase
- Official inflation says 20x, reality is 600x for housing

### Birth Rate Connection (Plamen's thesis)
- 1960s UK birth rate: ~2.5 children per woman
- 2025 UK birth rate: ~1.4 children per woman
- Housing unaffordability → delayed family formation → lower birth rates
- Wealth extraction from younger to older generation via mortgages
- System creates debt slavery that discourages reproduction

### Mining Area Subsidies
- Whitehaven was a coal mining area
- Council loans in mining areas made housing artificially cheap
- Even at subsidized prices, 6 months work = 1 house
- Today: 3.5-4 YEARS work = 1 house in same area
